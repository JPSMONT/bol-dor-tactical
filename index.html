Wind pattern guide — Lac Léman (ASLeman + PRD §11)
Synoptic
Bise (NE) — Cold, dry, 15–80 km/h. 3/6/9-day cycles. Strongest Petit Lac. Favours south (French) bank upwind.
Vent (SW) — Warm, variable 10–70 km/h. Often pre-frontal. Gusty near mountains; mid-lake usually steadiest.
Vaudaire (S/SE) — Dual: (1) foehn via Rhône valley — warm, sustained; (2) thunderstorm outflow — violent gusts >50 km/h. Dominates Haut-Lac.
Joran (NW) — Katabatic from Jura, often with thunderstorms. Rapid onset, dominates Petit Lac. Can reverse race dynamics.
Thermals (PRD §11.2)
Séchard (NE) — Light morning thermal, land-to-lake. Most common June morning breeze. Useful for return-leg setup.
Morget (S) — Afternoon thermal near Morges/Lausanne. Reliable 8–15 kn. Favours north (Swiss) bank in Grand Lac.
Bornan (S) — Thermal off the Savoyard Alps, French shore. Favours French bank in Grand Lac transit.
Fraidieu (NW) — Cold thermal from Jura, distinct from Joran: lighter, no thunderstorms. Gentle night breeze in Petit Lac.
Vauderon (variable) — Complex Vaud-region thermal. Localised, unpredictable, but useful in calm.
Môlan (S/SE) — Southerly thermal in Haut-Lac. Relevant for Le Bouveret approach and the return leg's first hour.

Tactical notes
Bol d'Or course: Start in Geneva, head ENE to Le Bouveret mark (Haut-Lac), return WSW to Geneva. ~62 NM total. First leg is the bank-selection leg — this is where races are won or lost.
Key decision at Morges: After clearing the Geneva basin, choose north bank (Swiss shore, closer to thermals and Morget) or south bank (French shore, often more Bise). The wind differential between banks can be 5+ knots.
Little Johnka's edge: CYD 27 excels in 8-14 kn TWS with VMG >4 kn upwind. In light air (<6 kn), keep the boat moving with minimal manoeuvres. Sail inventory: Main (27.1 m²), Jib (17.7 m²), Spinnaker (47.2 m²).
